Hey there! Let's talk about photosynthesis — it's the amazing process plants use to make their own food! Think of plants as tiny factories that run on sunlight. 🌞
🎓 Miss Lily
Plants take in carbon dioxide from the air through tiny holes in their leaves, and water from the soil through their roots. Inside the leaf, there are special structures called chloroplasts that contain a green pigment called chlorophyll.
🎓 Miss Lily
Chlorophyll captures the sun's energy and uses it to combine CO₂ and water into glucose — that's the plant's food! And guess what? They release oxygen as a byproduct. That's the air we breathe! 🌬️

💡 Real-World Analogy

Think of the chloroplast as a solar-powered kitchen. The light reactions are like the solar panels on the roof — they capture sunlight and convert it into electrical energy (ATP). Then the Calvin cycle is the chef inside the kitchen, using that energy to cook the food (glucose) from the ingredients (CO₂)!
